Projects

Transparent Reasoning with Large Language Models

Johns Hopkins Center for Language and Speech Processing. Advisor: Dr. Benjamin Van Durme. September 2024 - Present.

  • Researched social natural language inference to improve how LLMs interpret sarcasm, humor, and irony.
  • Created SocialNLI, a dialogue-centric social-inference dataset with 243 Friends TV transcripts, 5.3K generated inferences, and a 1.4K human-annotated eval split.
  • Developed a counterfactual-reasoning pipeline to assign calibrated plausibility scores to social inferences and surface LLM failure modes.
  • Constructed a taxonomy of language model failure modes on dialogue-centric data.
  • Leveraging token-level and rubric-based rewards to enhance reasoning processes in LLMs using reinforcement learning.

Nuss Bar

Johns Hopkins Laboratory for Computational Sensing and Robotics. Advisor: Peter Kazanzides. February 2024 - July 2024.

  • Created a desktop planning and visualization app to aid clinicians in shaping bars for the Nuss procedure.
  • Conceived a user study comparing augmented-reality Nuss bar prototypes against 3D printed bars.
  • Built an augmented reality workflow using Unity, C#, Python, Slicer3D, and MRTK3.

SurgiSimulate

Johns Hopkins Laboratory for Computational Sensing and Robotics. Advisor: Peter Kazanzides. February 2022 - July 2023.

  • Enabled remote surgical training by turning an iPhone into a handheld controller for the dVRK surgical robot.
  • Built a mobile app using Swift, Objective-C, ARKit, ROS, and Python for robot control.
  • Designed and ran a 16-participant user study validating feasibility for mobile teleoperation.
  • Presented research at the Hamlyn Symposium on Medical Robotics.
